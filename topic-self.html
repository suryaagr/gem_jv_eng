The Conscious Self (Jeevan)

Exploring Our Inner World

Limited Consciousness Active Faculties Shift in Perspective

The Problem of Limited Consciousness

The fundamental human problem is that we live in a state of Animal Consciousness (`Jeev Chetna`). In this state, only 4.5 of our 10 innate conscious activities are fully functional. The purpose of this self-exploration is to transition to Human Consciousness (`Manav Chetna`) by awakening the dormant, higher faculties of the Self.

The Currently Active Faculties of the Self

In the state of Animal Consciousness, our experience is dominated by the activities of three faculties:

  • Man: Governs our sensory preferences of "likes" and "dislikes."
  • Vritti: Responsible for our thought processes, comparing and analyzing situations.
  • Chitta: Where we hold our desires, ambitions, and assumptions, but without the guidance of correct understanding.

The Shift in Perspective

The key to transitioning from conflict to harmony lies in shifting the basis of our evaluation—our perspective (`drishti`)—from being limited and self-centered to being universal and conducive to mutual fulfillment.

Limited Perspectives (Source of Conflict) Resolved Perspectives (Source of Harmony)
Priy (Likes/Dislikes): Evaluation based on what is pleasant or unpleasant to the senses. Nyaay (Justice): Evaluation based on ensuring mutual fulfillment in human relationships.
Hit (Profit/Loss): Evaluation based on material gain, benefit, or loss. Dharma (Order): Evaluation based on conforming to the inherent harmony and orderliness in all of existence.
Labh (Health/Sensation): Evaluation based on what is favorable for the body or provides a pleasant sensation. Satya (Truth): Evaluation based on understanding reality as it truly is, free from assumptions.
