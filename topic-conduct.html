Foundational Values in Human Relationships

When we evaluate others based on the Self instead of the Body, certain values naturally establish themselves. The three most foundational are Trust, Respect, and Affection.

Trust (Vishwas)

Proposal: Trust is the foundational value, defined as the assurance that the other person's fundamental intention is to make me happy and prosperous.

Respect (Samman)

Proposal: Respect is defined as correct evaluation—seeing all humans as equal on the basis of the Self.

Affection (Sneh)

Proposal: Affection is the feeling of being related to another, which arises naturally from Respect.

Humane conduct extends beyond interpersonal relationships to our interaction with wealth and nature.

  • Wealth Ethics (Artha Neeti): Wealth should be for nourishment, not exploitation.
  • Behavioral Ethics (Vyavahar Neeti): The commitment to "Live and Let Live."
  • Natural Ethics (Prakriti Neeti): Fulfilling needs from nature in a way that ensures its enrichment and harmony.
