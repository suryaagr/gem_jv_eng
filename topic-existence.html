Existence as Coexistence: Units Submerged in Space

Existence (`Astitva`) is proposed to be the coexistence of two fundamental realities: Units (`Prakriti`) and Space (`Shunya` or `Vyapak`). Every "thing" we see is a Unit, and all units are submerged in Space.

Units are limited, active, and organized. Space is unlimited, inactive, and the source of all energy.

The Four Orders of Nature

All units in nature can be classified into four distinct orders, each with its own innate nature and inherent orderliness.

  1. Material Order (Padarth Avastha): Non-living matter. Innate nature is composition/decomposition.
  2. Plant/Bio Order (Pran Avastha): All flora. Innate nature is to enhance or worsen.
  3. Animal Order (Jeev Avastha): All fauna. Innate nature is brutality/gentleness.
  4. Human Order (Gyan Avastha): The order of knowledge. Awakened nature is steadfastness, courage, and generosity.

The Progression of Consciousness

The four orders represent a natural evolutionary progression. The material order provides the basis for the plant order, which provides the basis for the animal body, and so on. The emergence of the conscious Self (`Jeevan`) is a natural part of this development. The subsequent "awakening" of the Self's dormant higher faculties—the shift from Animal Consciousness to Human Consciousness—is the next logical and necessary stage in this grand evolutionary progression.
