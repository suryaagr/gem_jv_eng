The Foundational Premise

Understanding the Human Being

Self and Body Four Dimensions Ultimate Goal

The Human Being as a Coexistence of Self and Body

The core proposal is that a human is a coexistence of two distinct but interconnected realities: the Self (`Jeevan`) and the Body (`Sharir`). The Self is the entity that experiences, desires, and understands; the Body is the instrument the Self uses to interact with the material world.

Confusing these two realities leads to insecurity and misplaced effort. We anchor our sense of self in a temporary physical form, but our true needs—like happiness, trust, and respect—belong to the conscious Self.

Key Differences: Self vs. Body
Attribute The Self (Jeevan) The Body (Sharir)
Needs Happiness, Trust, Respect (qualitative) Food, Shelter, Clothing (quantitative)
Activities Desiring, Thinking, Believing, Knowing Breathing, Digestion, Heartbeat
Nature of Activities Continuous (e.g., desire for happiness is always present) Temporary (e.g., eating is for a limited time)
Response Knowing, Assuming, Recognizing, Fulfilling Recognizing, Fulfilling

The Four Dimensions of Human Living

Our experience of life unfolds across four interconnected dimensions. Conflict or harmony in any one of these affects all the others.

  1. Material (Bhautik): Interaction with physical reality, work, and nature.
  2. Behavioral (Vyavahar): Interaction with other human beings.
  3. Intellectual (Baudhik): The inner realm of thoughts, ideas, and beliefs.
  4. Spiritual (Adhyatmik): The direct experience of understanding reality as it is.

The Ultimate Goal: Comprehensive Happiness (Samadhan)

Happiness is "a state of non-conflict in every aspect of my living." It is a state of harmony and resolution across all four dimensions. Unhappiness is a state of conflict or contradiction.

Achieving a state of comprehensive happiness, or `Samadhan`, is the fundamental and universal goal of human life.
